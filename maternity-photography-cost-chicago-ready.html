How Much Does Maternity Photography Cost in Chicago? [2026 Price Guide]

A complete guide to maternity photography pricing — so you can choose what's right for you.

Pregnancy is such a beautiful, fleeting time. It makes sense that you want to capture it — and it makes sense that you want to understand what you're investing in.

If you've been researching maternity photographers in Chicago, you've probably noticed prices range anywhere from $200 to $1,500 or more. That's a big difference, and it can feel confusing.

Here's what we want you to know: there's no "right" or "wrong" price. Different prices simply reflect different experiences and different results.

Understanding what goes into each price point will help you make an informed choice — one that's right for you.

Understanding the Price Differences

Budget Sessions: $200–$400

At this price point, you typically get:

  • Outdoor sessions at parks or public locations
  • Shorter session time (30–60 minutes)
  • Basic photo editing (color correction, brightness)
  • Digital files delivered
  • You provide your own wardrobe

This can be a good option if you're looking for natural, casual photos and you have outfits you feel great in.

What to keep in mind: At this price, photographers generally can't include extras like studio space, wardrobe, or detailed retouching. These things have real costs that need to be covered somewhere.

Mid-Range Sessions: $400–$700

Here you'll often find:

  • Longer sessions (1–1.5 hours)
  • Studio or outdoor options
  • More thorough editing and basic retouching
  • More photos in your final gallery
  • Some photographers offer a limited selection of dresses

This is a nice middle ground — you get more time, more attention to detail, and often better editing.

Full-Service Sessions: $700–$1,500+

At this level, you typically receive:

  • Private studio with professional equipment
  • Full wardrobe access (designer gowns, accessories)
  • Complete posing guidance throughout your session
  • Professional retouching on selected images
  • Family members and pets included
  • A comprehensive experience from start to finish
Why the higher investment? Because providing this level of service requires significant ongoing costs — studio rent, wardrobe collection, professional equipment, years of specialized training, and time for detailed retouching on every image.

Comparison: What Your Experience Actually Looks Like

To make the difference clearer, imagine two scenarios:

Scenario A: The $350 Session

You arrange to meet at a park. You arrive in your own dress — something you found in your closet or bought specially. You hope it photographs well.

The photographer shoots for 30–40 minutes. You're not quite sure how to pose — you do what feels right. If it's windy, cold, or too hot outside — you deal with it. There's nowhere to change.

A week later, you receive 20–30 photos. The editing is basic — colors adjusted, brightness tweaked. The things that bother you in photos (skin, poses, small details) — they stay as they are.

Total: $350 for the session itself. But if you add a dress ($100–200) and a makeup artist ($100–150) — you're already at $550–700.

Scenario B: The $800 Session

You arrive at a private studio. You're greeted warmly. Your partner can relax in the lounge area.

You walk over to the wardrobe and choose from dozens of designer gowns — those same ones you've been saving on Pinterest. You didn't need to buy or search for anything.

Two hours of shooting. The photographer guides every pose: where to turn your head, how to place your hands, what expression to make. You don't need to think — just follow the directions and relax.

Your favorite music plays in the studio. There's a private space to change. No strangers around.

Two weeks later, you receive 150+ photos. 15 of them have professional retouching: skin looks flawless, everything that bothered you has been carefully addressed. The photos look like they're from a magazine.

Total: $800 — and you don't need to buy anything else. Everything is included.

What You're Actually Paying For

It helps to understand what goes into professional maternity photography:

A Dedicated Studio

A private space in Chicago means:

  • Complete privacy for outfit changes
  • Professional lighting designed for flattering results
  • Professional equipment
  • Lounge areas for guests
  • A calm, comfortable environment just for you
  • No worrying about weather, crowds, or finding parking

Maintaining a professional studio in Chicago is a significant monthly expense for any photographer.

A Curated Wardrobe Collection

Those beautiful flowing gowns you see in maternity portraits? Quality designer gowns for photography cost $300–$800 each. Building and maintaining a full collection is a serious investment of tens of thousands of dollars.

When wardrobe is included in your session, you don't need to worry about finding something that photographs well or spending money on a dress you'll wear once.

Professional Retouching

Basic editing: Adjusting brightness, contrast, and color — quick and efficient. Takes a few minutes per photo.

Professional retouching: Careful, detailed work on each image — natural skin smoothing, refining details, body shaping so you look your absolute best. This takes 30–40 minutes per photo and requires real skill.

With professional retouching, your photographer can address anything that might bother you in photos — so you can simply enjoy the results.

Years of Specialization

A photographer who specializes in maternity sessions knows:

  • How to pose you in the most flattering ways for your body and stage of pregnancy
  • How to work with a changing body
  • How to help you feel comfortable and relaxed
  • How to create images that feel timeless, not just trendy
  • How to naturally involve partners and children

This expertise comes from years of focused practice — not something you learn in a couple of months.

The question to ask yourself: what am I receiving for that time?

How to Know What You're Really Getting

Words like "luxury" and "premium" are easy to use. What matters is what's actually behind them.

Here are some simple questions that can help you see the full picture:

About the studio:

  • Is there an actual studio address you can visit?
  • Is it a dedicated photography space or someone's home/living room?
  • Will you have complete privacy for outfit changes?
  • Is there a lounge area for your partner and kids?

About the wardrobe:

  • Can you see ALL available dresses — not just one or two photos?
  • Are they designer gowns or basic options?
  • How many choices will you have?
  • How often is the collection updated?

About retouching:

  • Will they work on skin texture, body shaping, small details?
  • Can you see before/after examples of their retouching work?

About experience:

  • How many years has the photographer specialized in maternity?
  • Can you see a full portfolio (not just the best 5 photos)?
  • What do reviews say about the actual experience?

A photographer with genuine premium service will happily answer all of these questions.
